Modeling a hospital ED patient flow as a queueing network to find where
wait times blow up. Found the bottleneck is not triage but imaging
turnaround; adding one tech to the imaging stage cuts the 90th-percentile
wait more than adding two triage nurses. The lesson is that the constraint
governs throughput, so improving non-bottleneck stages barely moves the
overall metric.
