Final paper argues that time-of-use electricity pricing alone underdelivers
on demand shifting for residential customers without automation, because
manual response fatigues. The interesting result is the interaction effect:
TOU pricing plus smart-thermostat automation gets most of the load-shifting
benefit, while pricing alone gets a fraction. Need to find a dataset with
both pricing variation and device-level automation to support this.
